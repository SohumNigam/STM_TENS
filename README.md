

STM_TENS aims to solve a design flaw in current nerve stimuation devices.



PROBLEM: 

Commercial TENS units rely on user based adjustments to deliver proper stimlutation.
This can often resilt in over/under stimlulation and subsequently, discomfort.
Furthermore, variables such as temparature, subject age, and skin moisture can all affect skin conductivity.
Leaving these variables unknown to the unit can lead to wildly incosistent stimulation, even throughout a single session.

This project aims to take measurment from the patients skin to constantly update the skin stimulation leading to a more confortable and cosistent pain relief experience.


PROJECT REQUIRMENTS:

STM_TENS should be able to...

1. Generate a precise, high voltage biphasic signal to stimulate pain reception fibers at a predetermined, adjustable frequency. (Just like commercial tens)
2. Measure the current being sent through the skin at the exact moment of a singnal pulse. (Unlike commercial tens)
3. Adjust the biphasic signal to account for inconsistencies in skin impedance. (Unlike commercial tens)
